SINCE 1949...

Fender, one of the most iconic names in the music industry, has a rich history that dates back to the early 1940s. The company was founded by Leo Fender in Fullerton, California, USA. Leo's fascination with electronics and tinkering led him to create his first solid-body electric guitar in 1949, the Fender Esquire.



The Esquire was followed by the famous Fender Telecaster, released in 1951. This guitar quickly gained popularity due to its solid construction and unique tonal qualities. A few years later, Leo introduced another revolutionary model, the Fender Stratocaster, in 1954. The Stratocaster's sleek design, versatile sound, and ergonomic playability made it a favorite among musicians across genres.



In addition to guitars, Fender also delved into the world of bass guitars. In 1951, they launched the Fender Precision Bass, which was the world's first commercially successful electric bass guitar. This instrument revolutionized bass playing, providing a more defined sound and easier handling for bassists.



Fender's innovations didn't stop there. In 1958, they introduced the Fender Jazzmaster, aiming to cater to jazz musicians. Though not an instant commercial success, it later gained popularity among alternative and indie rock artists in the following decades.



The 1960s marked a period of great expansion for Fender. They acquired a new manufacturing facility in Corona, California, and introduced amplifiers and other musical instruments to their product line. Fender amps, like the iconic Fender Twin Reverb, became synonymous with legendary guitar tones, used by countless artists across the years.



Despite Leo Fender's decision to sell the company to CBS in 1965, the Fender brand continued to thrive. In the early 1980s, a group of Fender employees, led by William Schultz, bought the company back from CBS, returning it to private ownership and ensuring a renewed focus on quality and innovation.



Throughout the decades, Fender has released numerous guitar and bass models, collaborated with renowned artists for signature instruments, and expanded its product offerings to meet the demands of musicians worldwide.



Today, Fender remains a dominant force in the music industry, producing guitars, basses, amplifiers, and accessories that continue to shape the sound of contemporary music. With a legacy spanning over seven decades, Fender's influence on popular music culture is undeniable, and its instruments continue to be cherished by musicians of all levels.
