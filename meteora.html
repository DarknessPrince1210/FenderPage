METEORA...?

The Fender Meteora is a unique electric guitar model that was introduced by the iconic guitar manufacturer Fender in 2018. With its striking and distinctive design, the Fender Meteora quickly captured the attention of guitar enthusiasts and players alike. Its creation was a testament to Fender's willingness to experiment and push the boundaries of guitar design while maintaining its commitment to quality craftsmanship and playability.



The history of the Fender Meteora can be traced back to the mid-1950s when Fender was already establishing itself as a pioneer in the electric guitar industry. During this time, the company produced iconic models such as the Stratocaster and the Telecaster, which would go on to become timeless classics. However, Fender's innovative spirit did not stop there, and they continued to experiment with new designs.



The initial concept for the Meteora dates back to the late 1950s when the legendary guitar designer and builder George Fullerton collaborated with Fender. Together, they explored various unconventional body shapes and contours, some of which were ahead of their time. However, many of these experimental designs were shelved in favor of more traditional models that resonated with the public.



Fast forward to the 2010s, and Fender decided to revisit some of its forgotten design concepts from the past. The Meteora's distinctive double-cutaway offset body shape with angular edges was resurrected, showcasing the guitar's retro-futuristic appearance. This design is a nod to the early explorations in guitar design and also embodies elements of modern aesthetics.



In 2018, the Fender Meteora was officially released as part of the company's Parallel Universe series. The series was characterized by the reimagining and blending of classic Fender designs to create new, innovative instruments. The Meteora fit perfectly into this series as it combined the sleek contours of the offset body with vintage-inspired features, such as a hardtail bridge and a choice of traditional single-coil or humbucking pickups.



The Fender Meteora's construction and materials are in line with Fender's commitment to quality. It features an alder body, which is a tonewood commonly used in Fender guitars for its balanced tonal characteristics. The bolt-on maple neck contributes to the guitar's bright and snappy tone, while the choice of rosewood or maple fretboard offers players different tonal options.



Despite its relatively recent introduction, the Fender Meteora has gained popularity among players across various genres. Its unique design and tonal versatility make it an appealing choice for guitarists seeking something different from the more traditional Fender models.



In conclusion, the Fender Meteora represents a fascinating chapter in the company's history, where they revisited and brought to life an innovative design concept from the past. With its bold aesthetic and versatile sound, the Meteora stands as a testament to Fender's commitment to pushing boundaries and creating instruments that inspire musicians to explore new sonic territories. Whether on stage or in the studio, the Fender Meteora continues to make its mark in the world of electric guitars.
